# R24 Matched-Closure Acquisition Skeptical Review

Scope: custody of the existing production V5 bundle and exact external-closure products. No real
Hermes or Paperclip campaign, independent unit, fault administration, or external authority ran.

## Claims that survive review

- Preregistration precedes and cryptographically constrains the atomic V5 matched bundle. Acquisition
  does not reinterpret its internal plan, cells, transcripts, source custody, grading, accounting, or
  analysis layers.
- The immutable bundle induces exactly five substrate-lock equivalence witnesses per pair and a
  canonical flattened difference inventory. The complete fixture yields 40 equivalence cells and
  1,832 triage cells; five dependency closures are also exact and mandatory.
- Preregistration, bundle custody, equivalence, triage, closure, and all five dependency keys are
  cryptographically distinct. The bundle itself retains the additional independent planner, result,
  grader, accounting, and source-custody roles checked by the production verifier.
- Triage cannot begin before all equivalence witnesses. Final closure cannot begin before every
  equivalence, difference, and dependency cell is accepted.
- Large-fragment causal binding uses the signed response-envelope digest, which commits to the request,
  fragment digest, signer, timestamp, and signature without repeatedly hashing the embedded bundle.
- Persisted state rederives the bundle-induced domains once, then revalidates every request, response,
  authority, predecessor, candidate digest, and final assembly.
- A complete 8-pair, 2-unit, 4-repetition, 2-fault fixture reconstructs exactly through acquisition and
  is accepted by the production R24 external-closure verifier.

## Exercised attacks

Tests reject stage skipping, unregistered or missing dependencies, dependency substitution, wrong
signers, forged envelopes, equivocation, cryptographic key aliases, and persisted request/domain drift.
CLI issuance is durable before request exposure.

## Residual limitations

1. Acquisition proves custody, not that bundle observations or triage rationales are externally true.
2. The positive fixture is synthetic; no live Hermes/Paperclip matched campaign was executed here.
3. One CLI writer is assumed per state path; issued immutable requests may execute concurrently.
4. R24 remains blocked on closed dependencies and genuine external execution.

## Decision

The protocol is ready to acquire a real R24 closure campaign but proves no R24 closure by itself.
