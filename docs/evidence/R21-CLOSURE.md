# R21 twin-conformant engineering closure

R21 closes reliability engineering within the declared deterministic bench-world envelope. Eight runtime service
classes have dimensioned SLO/error-budget evaluation, bounded queues, tenant reservations, dominant-resource
fairness, load shedding, degradation modes, and causal fault cuts. Authenticated recovery preserves revocations and
acknowledged effects across controller and filesystem reconstruction; maintenance, schema, version, credential, drain,
and decommission transitions are checked.

The campaign uses virtual time, controlled load and operator actors, deterministic service/environment fault models,
and signed evidence. It proves invariants and bounds for the declared world. It does not claim independently deployed
multi-region capacity, provider billing/KMS custody, unfamiliar-human performance, or production disaster recovery.
Those remain additive external-validation claims.
